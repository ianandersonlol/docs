Most of the time, one Claude working through your task step by step is exactly right. But every so often you hit a job that's *sprawling* — comb through every contract in a folder, cross-check a pile of research, leave absolutely nothing out — and you'd happily trade some cost for thoroughness. That's what **ultracode** is for. It's the "go big" button.

<Note>
  **The simple version:** ultracode tells Claude to stop working alone and instead put a whole *team* of itself on your task, all at once, to be exhaustive. It's powerful — and it uses a lot more of your plan's usage. Reach for it when "complete and thorough" matters more than "quick and cheap."
</Note>

## What it actually does

Ultracode flips on two things at the same time:

1. **It cranks the thinking up** to a high effort level (see [Choosing a Model & Effort](/agentic-ai/claude-code/best-practices/choosing-a-model)).
2. **It fans the work out across many helper agents** running in parallel — instead of one Claude doing everything in sequence, it's a coordinated team, each tackling a slice, with the results stitched back together.

The analogy that makes it click: normally you've got one sharp analyst working a list of 200 documents one at a time. With ultracode, you put a whole team on it — each person takes a stack, a coordinator hands out the work, and a reviewer double-checks the findings before they reach you. Same task, far more coverage, finished as one tidy answer.

## When it earns its keep

Ultracode shines on **big, sprawling jobs where completeness is the whole point:**

- **Combing a large pile.** *"Go through every contract in the `inputs` folder and flag each one missing a signature or an end date."*
- **Heavy, repetitive work across many files.** Standardizing, extracting, or cross-referencing a hundred documents at once.
- **Deep research that checks itself.** Fan out across many sources, then have agents verify each other's claims before you trust them.
- **Looking at a problem from several angles.** Draft a plan from a few independent perspectives, then keep the best of each.

If a task makes you think *"this would take a person all afternoon and they'd still miss a few,"* it's an ultracode candidate.

## How to turn it on

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="For one task (recommended)">
    Just put the word **`ultracode`** in your request. Claude uses the big-team approach for that task only, then goes back to normal.

    > ultracode: go through every PDF in the `inputs` folder and build me one spreadsheet listing each document's title, date, total amount, and counterparty.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="For a whole session">
    Type **`/effort ultracode`** to leave it on for everything that follows. Switch back with **`/effort high`** when you're done with the heavy lifting.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## The trade-off: thoroughness costs usage

There's no free lunch. Spinning up a team means doing a lot more work under the hood, so a single ultracode task can use **many times** the plan usage of doing it the ordinary way.

| | Normal | Ultracode |
|---|---|---|
| How it works | One Claude, step by step | A whole team of Claudes, in parallel |
| Thoroughness | Great for most tasks | Exhaustive — built to leave nothing out |
| Speed to finish | Quick to get going | Slower to spin up, but covers far more ground |
| Usage cost | Normal | **Much higher** — potentially several times a normal task |
| Best for | Day-to-day work | Big, sprawling, must-be-complete jobs |

<Warning>
  Ultracode is a sledgehammer. For a quick question, a small edit, or anything a single pass handles well, it's overkill — you'll burn through your plan's usage for no real benefit. Match the tool to the task.
</Warning>

## When *not* to use it

- Quick, simple, or one-off tasks.
- Anything you can comfortably check in a minute.
- When you're still exploring and not sure what you want yet — figure that out cheaply first, *then* go big if needed.

## One naming gotcha

<Note>
  Don't confuse **ultracode** with **`/code-review ultra`** (sometimes called "ultrareview"). They sound alike but are different tools: ultracode puts a team on *doing your task*, while `/code-review ultra` is a separate, developer-focused feature for deeply reviewing code changes. For general spreadsheet-and-document work, ultracode is the one you'll reach for — you can ignore the other.
</Note>
