Claude Code is built to be safe by default: the beginner workflow starts from a folder you choose, and Claude asks permission before doing things that matter. Add a couple of simple habits on top and you can work with confidence.

## The two built-in guardrails

1. **The workspace boundary.** Claude starts inside the [workspace folder](/agentic-ai/claude-code/first-session/workspace) you opened. If you add another folder, connect a tool, or approve broader access later, treat that as a deliberate expansion.
2. **Permission prompts.** Before Claude runs a command, edits a file, or creates something, it asks. Nothing meaningful happens without your say-so.

## Reading a permission prompt

When Claude wants to act, you'll see a prompt describing what it intends to do. Your options:

| Choice | Meaning | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| **Allow once** | Approve just this action | Your default while learning — keeps you in the loop |
| **Allow always** (this kind, this project) | Stop asking for this *type* of action here | A repetitive, clearly-safe action you've seen enough times to trust |
| **Reject** | Don't do it | Anything that looks wrong, or touches something you didn't intend |

<Tip>
  Read the prompt before clicking. The point of the prompt isn't to rubber-stamp — it's to give you a beat to notice "wait, why is it deleting that?" The actions worth pausing on are anything that **deletes**, **overwrites**, or reaches **outside your folder**.
</Tip>

## Safety habits worth adopting

<Steps>
  <Step title="Work on copies of anything important">
    Drag a *copy* of that critical file into your workspace, never your only original. Then the worst case is "redo it," never "lose it." This one habit removes most of the risk on its own.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Keep an undo path">
    Know how you'd undo. The simplest version: you worked on a copy, so your original is safe. Claude Code can also rewind to checkpoints in many sessions, and Git gives you full version history for longer projects. See [Undo & Rewind](/agentic-ai/claude-code/best-practices/undo-and-rewind).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start cautious, loosen over time">
    New to a project? Approve actions one at a time. As you learn what Claude does and trust it, you can switch the safe, repetitive stuff to "Allow always." Earn the trust, then grant it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Handling sensitive or confidential data

If you work in finance, legal, or anywhere with confidential material, treat Claude Code like any other cloud tool — because to do its job, your prompts and the files it reads are sent to Anthropic's models to be processed.

- **Follow your organization's policy.** If your company has rules about what can go into AI tools, those apply here. When in doubt, ask before loading a data room into a workspace.
- **Prefer approved environments for regulated data.** Team and Enterprise plans exist partly for this; your IT or compliance team may have a sanctioned setup.
- **Practice on non-sensitive material.** While you're learning the ropes, use data you wouldn't mind sharing. Save the confidential work for when you're confident *and* cleared.

<Warning>
  Claude can't tell whether a document is public or a sealed M&A agreement — only you can. The responsibility for *what* you put in front of it is always yours.
</Warning>

## The bottom line

The workspace boundary plus permission prompts make Claude Code safer by design. Working on copies and knowing your undo path make it safer in practice. Do those, and you can approve ordinary actions without holding your breath.
