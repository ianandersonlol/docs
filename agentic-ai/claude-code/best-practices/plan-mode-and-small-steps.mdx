Two habits prevent the large majority of beginner mishaps: making Claude **plan before it acts**, and breaking big tasks into **small, checkable steps**. Neither requires any technical skill — just a little discipline.

## Look before you leap

For anything beyond a trivial request, get Claude's plan *before* it does anything. It's the cheapest possible insurance: catching a misunderstanding in a plan costs nothing; catching it after Claude has rewritten ten files costs you cleanup.

The plain-English version always works:

> Before you change anything, tell me your plan — which files you'll touch and what you'll do to each. Don't act yet.

Read the plan like a contract. If it says it'll overwrite your original file and you didn't want that, you just saved yourself a headache — for free.

### Plan Mode

Claude Code also has a dedicated **Plan Mode**: a setting where Claude will *research and propose* but make **no changes** until you explicitly approve. It's like asking a contractor for a written estimate before any work begins.

- In the **desktop app**, you can switch a session into Plan Mode from the session's mode controls.
- In the **terminal**, you cycle interaction modes with the **Shift+Tab** keys until you reach plan mode.

If you can't find the toggle, don't worry — the plain-English "tell me your plan first, don't act yet" gets you 90% of the benefit and works everywhere.

<Tip>
  Use Plan Mode whenever the task touches important files, involves many steps, or you're just not sure what Claude will do. When in doubt, plan first.
</Tip>

## Work in small steps

The instinct is to describe the entire job in one giant paragraph. Resist it. A big, all-at-once request is hard for *you* to review and easy for Claude to get subtly wrong somewhere in the middle.

Compare:

<CodeGroup>
```text One giant ask
Clean this data, fix the dates, remove duplicates, calculate
monthly and quarterly totals, make three different charts,
write a summary memo, and format it all into a report.
```

```text Small steps
1. First, clean the data and show me what you removed.
   (check the result)
2. Now give me monthly totals as a table.
   (check the numbers)
3. Now make a bar chart of those.
   (look at the chart)
4. Now draft a one-page memo around it.
```
</CodeGroup>

The small-steps version takes the same total time but you **catch problems early**, while they're cheap to fix. If step 1 misread a column, you find out before it has polluted the charts and the memo.

## Checkpoints: confirm, then continue

Between steps, take a beat to verify before moving on. A natural way to keep Claude in step-by-step mode:

> Do that one step, show me the result, and wait for me before doing the next thing.

This keeps you in the driver's seat and turns a scary "it did a bunch of stuff" into a series of small, reviewed moves.

## The rule of thumb

> If you couldn't easily check the result in under a minute, the step is too big. Break it down.
