This is the most universal business win, so it's the one to do first. You have a messy export of data; you want it cleaned, summarized, and turned into a chart — without spending an hour fighting Excel formulas.

**What you'll practice:** the [core loop](/agentic-ai/claude-code/first-session/core-loop), reading a plan, approving steps, and verifying numbers.

## What you'll need

- A [spreadsheet file](/agentic-ai/claude-code/glossary) (a `.csv` or `.xlsx`) with some data in it — sales, expenses, a list of transactions, anything with numbers and dates. A messy one is *better* for practice.
- A [workspace folder](/agentic-ai/claude-code/first-session/workspace) with a **copy** of that file in it.

<Note>
  **Want to follow along with the exact same file?** This site ships a deliberately messy sample at `agentic-ai/claude-code/examples/sample-sales.csv`. Grab it from the repo and drop a copy in your workspace folder — it's ~60 rows of fake sales with duplicate lines, three different date formats, and a few blank amounts. The mess is the point. A peek at what you're walking into:

  ```csv sample-sales.csv
  date,customer,product,amount,region
  2025-03-04,Acme Corp,Consulting Hours,1640.00,North
  03/04/2025,Acme Corp,Consulting Hours,1640.00,North
  January 9 2025,Initech LLC,Consulting Hours,1875.50,East
  01/24/2025,Soylent Corp,Consulting Hours,,East
  ```
</Note>

<Tip>
  No data handy and don't want to download anything? Just ask Claude: *"Create a sample CSV of 500 fake sales transactions with some duplicates and messy dates so I can practice."* Now you have a realistic mess to clean.
</Tip>

## The walkthrough

<Steps>
  <Step title="Let Claude look first">
    Before asking for anything, have Claude describe what it's working with:

    > Take a look at `sales.csv` and tell me what's in it — how many rows, what the columns are, and anything messy you notice (duplicates, blanks, inconsistent formats).

    Claude reads the file and reports back. You'll often learn things about your own data here ("three different date formats," "112 duplicate rows").
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ask for the cleanup — as a new file">
    Now the real request. Notice we tell it to save a **new** file rather than overwrite the original:

    > Clean this up: remove duplicate rows, standardize the dates to YYYY-MM-DD, and drop rows with no amount. Save the result as `sales-clean.csv` — don't change the original. Tell me what you removed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approve the steps">
    Claude will propose what it's doing and ask permission to run and to write the new file. Read each prompt, approve with **Allow once**. When it finishes, it'll summarize what changed ("removed 112 duplicates, fixed 340 dates, dropped 5 empty rows").
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ask for the summary and chart">
    > Using the cleaned data, give me a table of total revenue by month, and save a bar chart of it as `revenue-by-month.png`.

    Claude builds the table in the chat and saves a chart image into your folder. Open it like any image.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Verify before you trust it

This is the most important step, and the easiest to skip. A few quick checks:

- **Eyeball the chart.** Do the months and rough magnitudes make sense? A spike or a missing month is worth questioning.
- **Spot-check one number.** Pick one month and ask: *"Show me how you calculated March's total — which rows went into it?"* Claude will show its work.
- **Sanity-check the totals.** *"What's the grand total across all months, and does it match the sum of the cleaned file?"*

<Warning>
  "It produced a chart" is not "the chart is right." Claude is capable but can misread a column or make an assumption you didn't intend. Thirty seconds of spot-checking is what makes this trustworthy. See [Reviewing & Verifying](/agentic-ai/claude-code/best-practices/reviewing-and-verifying).
</Warning>

## Take it further

Once the basics work, try iterating — this is where it gets fun:

- *"Make it quarterly instead of monthly."*
- *"Add a line for a 3-month rolling average."*
- *"Break it out by region in a grouped bar chart."*
- *"Put the table and chart into a one-page summary I can send to my boss."*

Each of these is just another lap around the loop.
