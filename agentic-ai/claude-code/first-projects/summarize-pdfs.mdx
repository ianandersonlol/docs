This is the one that feels like a superpower the first time. You have a folder of PDFs — vendor proposals, contracts, research reports, a data room's worth of documents — and a question you need answered *across all of them*. Reading each one and copy-pasting into a chat window would take all afternoon. Claude reads the whole stack.

**What you'll practice:** working across many files at once, building comparison tables, and tracing claims back to the source.

## What you'll need

- A [workspace folder](/agentic-ai/claude-code/first-session/workspace) with several PDFs in it. Proposals, contracts, or reports work great.

<Tip>
  Keep the documents non-confidential while you're learning, or use your organization's approved environment for sensitive material. See [Permissions & Safety](/agentic-ai/claude-code/best-practices/permissions-and-safety) for handling confidential data responsibly.
</Tip>

## The walkthrough

<Steps>
  <Step title="Get the lay of the land">
    > List the PDFs in this folder and give me a one-sentence description of what each one is.

    This confirms Claude can read them and orients it before the heavy lifting.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ask for per-document summaries">
    > For each PDF, give me a short summary: the key points, any dollar figures, and important dates or deadlines. Keep each to a few bullets.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build a comparison across documents">
    The real payoff is comparing them side by side:

    > Now put these into a comparison table — one row per proposal — with columns for vendor, total price, contract length, and any termination clause. Save it as `proposal-comparison.csv`.

    Claude reads across every document and assembles the table. What was an afternoon is now a minute.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ask the question you actually care about">
    > Which proposal is cheapest over three years once you account for the annual increases? Show your math.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Verify — especially here

PDF extraction is powerful but it's where a confident-but-wrong answer can sneak in (a misread number, a clause pulled from the wrong section). So trace before you trust:

- **Ask for sources.** *"For each price in the table, tell me which document and roughly which page it came from."* Then open one or two and confirm.
- **Check the outlier.** If one vendor looks suspiciously cheap, that's exactly the figure to verify by hand.
- **Re-read the conclusion's basis.** *"What assumptions did you make to pick the cheapest option?"* Make sure they match reality.

<Warning>
  The more documents involved, the more tempting it is to trust the tidy table and skip checking. Resist that. Spot-check the numbers that decisions will hang on. See [Reviewing & Verifying](/agentic-ai/claude-code/best-practices/reviewing-and-verifying).
</Warning>

## Take it further

- *"Draft a one-page memo recommending a vendor, with the comparison table embedded."*
- *"Flag any contract clauses that are unusual or worth a lawyer's eye."*
- *"Pull every deadline across all documents into a single calendar list."*
