Everyone has the folder. A junk drawer of downloads — invoices, contracts, screenshots, scans — with names like `Scan_002 (final) FINAL (2).pdf`. This project shows the "an agent can operate your computer" idea at its most tangible, and it's wonderfully low-stakes.

**What you'll practice:** approving file operations carefully, planning before acting, and working safely on copies.

## What you'll need

- A folder full of mixed, messily-named files. (If you'd rather practice safely, copy a sampling of files into a fresh `practice-folder` first.)

<Warning>
  This project **moves and renames real files**. Do it on a **copy** the first time. Make a duplicate of the messy folder and point Claude at the duplicate — then if anything surprises you, your original is untouched.
</Warning>

## The walkthrough

<Steps>
  <Step title="Have Claude survey the mess">
    > Look at every file in this folder and tell me what's here — group them by what they seem to be (invoices, contracts, images, etc.) and point out anything with a confusing name.

    Claude inventories the folder and proposes natural groupings. You'll get a clear picture before a single file moves.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ask for a plan — not action yet">
    This is the key move. Get the plan in writing *before* anything happens:

    > Propose a clean folder structure and a consistent naming scheme for these files. Show me the full before-and-after list of what you'd rename and where you'd move it. **Don't move anything yet** — just show me the plan.

    Claude lays out exactly what it intends to do. Read it like a contract.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Adjust the plan in plain English">
    Tweak anything you don't like:

    > Use `YYYY-MM-Vendor` for invoice names, and put contracts in a separate `Contracts` subfolder. Otherwise looks good.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approve the moves">
    Now tell it to go. Claude will ask permission as it renames and moves files — approve with **Allow once** and watch the first few go by so you trust the pattern.

    > Great, go ahead and apply the plan.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Verify

- **Open the folder yourself** and look. Does the new structure match the plan?
- **Count.** *"How many files were there before, and how many are there now? Did anything get skipped or lost?"* The counts should reconcile.
- **Check a couple of renames** against the originals to be sure nothing was mislabeled.

<Tip>
  Because you worked on a copy, the safety net is built in: if the result isn't what you wanted, delete the copy and try again with a clearer instruction. Nothing was ever at risk.
</Tip>

## Take it further

- *"Also extract the invoice total and date from each PDF and put them in a spreadsheet."* (This bridges into the [PDF project](/agentic-ai/claude-code/first-projects/summarize-pdfs).)
- *"Flag any duplicates — same content, different names."*
- *"Write a short note explaining the folder structure so the next person understands it."*
